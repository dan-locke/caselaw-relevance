General Information

Assessing documents
Topics consist of an area of law from a decision of the United States Supreme Court. Each topic is the question asked in an appeal to the United States Supreme Court.

Several queries will be issued on loading the page. This may take some time (up to around a minute). A list of these queries are available in the search tab.

From there, further searches are required as you see fit. Please aim for a minimum of X documents per topic.

When assessing documents, there are the following relevance levels:
  • not relevant;
  • background;
  • explanatory; and
  • on point.
Tagging
Please tag documents where the text is relevant to the topic, as per the relevance level you select for the document.

You can tag by selecting the text in the judgment and pressing the tag button in the tags tab, or by pressing the key 't'.

Search
Search operators follow the LexisNexis style and precedence. The following operators are suported, in the following precedence:
  • OR
  • w/n
  • w/s
  • w/p
  • AND
  • NOT
You can use w/s and w/p in combinination with w/n operators. This is because, w/s and w/p default to a proximity of 20 and 50 terms respectively.

Terms will not match their derivations. ie. car. will not match care or cared. To do this, you must use the prefix operators ('!').

Wildcard operators are supported in clauses that do not involve proximity operators. Both "*" and "!" are supported as wildcard operators. Two instances are applicable:
  • "car*" or "car!" - in which case, the term, "car", denotes a prefix, and any terms that have the term as its prefix are matched - ie. car, carried, carrier;
  • "car*d" - in which case, the asterisk denotes any number of characters that may be matched before the prefix and suffix are to be matched.
A maximum of 20 new results are returned for a search, in which duplicates of already returned documents are excluded.

For more details, see the documentation of the query parser.
